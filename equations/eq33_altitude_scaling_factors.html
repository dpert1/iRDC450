Equation 33: Sachs Scaling Factors

Equations:

\[S_p = \frac{P}{P_0}\]
\[S_d = \left(\frac{P_0}{P}\right)^{1/3}\]
\[S_t = \left(\frac{P_0}{P}\right)^{1/3} \left(\frac{T_0}{T}\right)^{1/2}\]

Variables:

  • \(S_p\): Pressure scaling factor [-]
  • \(S_d\): Distance scaling factor [-]
  • \(S_t\): Time scaling factor [-]
  • \(P\): Ambient pressure [Pa]
  • \(P_0\): Reference pressure (sea level) [Pa]
  • \(T\): Ambient temperature [K]
  • \(T_0\): Reference temperature [K]

Description:

Sachs scaling factors account for atmospheric pressure and temperature variations when scaling blast effects to different altitudes or conditions.
