Equation 28: Radiant Exposure (cm units)

Equation:

\[Q \left[\frac{\text{cal}}{\text{cm}^2}\right] = \frac{10^{12} f W \tau}{4\pi D^2[\text{cm}]}\]

Variables:

  • \(Q\): Radiant exposure [cal/cm2]
  • \(f\): Thermal fraction of yield [-]
  • \(W\): Weapon yield [kt]
  • \(\tau\): Atmospheric transmission factor [-]
  • \(D\): Distance from burst [cm]

Description:

Thermal energy exposure at a given distance in units of calories per square centimeter, accounting for atmospheric transmission losses. The thermal fraction can be looked up in Glasstone Table 7.88 and the atmospheric transmission factor in figure 7.98.
