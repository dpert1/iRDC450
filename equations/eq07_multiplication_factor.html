Equation 7: Multiplication Factor

Equations:

\[k_\infty = \nu \frac{\Sigma_f}{\Sigma_a} = \nu \frac{\sigma_f}{\sigma_a}\]
\[k = k_\infty L\]

Variables:

  • \(k_\infty\): Infinite multiplication factor [-]
  • \(\nu\): Average number of neutrons per fission [-]
  • \(\Sigma_f\): Macroscopic fission cross section [cm-1]
  • \(\Sigma_a\): Macroscopic absorption cross section [cm-1]
  • \(\sigma_f\): Microscopic fission cross section [barns]
  • \(\sigma_a\): Microscopic absorption cross section [barns]
  • \(k\): Effective multiplication factor [-]
  • \(L\): Non-leakage probability [-]

Description:

The infinite multiplication factor represents neutron multiplication in an infinite medium. The effective multiplication factor accounts for neutron leakage in finite systems.
