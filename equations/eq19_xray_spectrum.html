Equation 19: X-Ray Spectrum

Equation:

\[S_x(h\nu) = Y_{ST} P(h\nu, T_{ST}) + Y_{RR} P(h\nu, T_{RR})\]

Variables:

  • \(S_x(h\nu)\): Total x-ray spectrum per photon energy [J/keV]
  • \(Y_{ST}\): Shine through x-ray yield [J]
  • \(Y_{RR}\): Case re-radiation x-ray yield [J]
  • \(P(h\nu, T)\): Normalized Planckian spectrum at temperature T [1/keV]
  • \(h\nu\): Photon energy [keV]
  • \(T_{ST}\): Shine through temperature [K]
  • \(T_{RR}\): Case re-radiation temperature [K]

Description:

The total X-ray spectrum from a nuclear explosion is a combination of the shine through and case re-radiation x-ray components. They are found by multiplying the yield of each component by its respective Planckian spectrum.
