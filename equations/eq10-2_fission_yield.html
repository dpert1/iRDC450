Equation 10-2: Energy Yield

Equations:

\[Y(t) = \frac{\Sigma_f v Q n_0}{\alpha} \left(e^{\alpha t} - 1\right)\]

Variables:

  • \(Y(t)\): Energy yield at time t [MeV or kT]
  • \(\Sigma_f\): Macroscopic fission cross section [cm-1]
  • \(v\): Neutron speed [cm/s]
  • \(Q\): Energy per fission [MeV or kT]
  • \(n_0\): Initial neutron population [neutrons]
  • \(\alpha\): Multiplication rate (bomb alpha) [s-1]
  • \(t\): Time [s]

Description:

Energy yield describes the exponential growth (or decay) of accumulated energy yield in a fissioning system over time.
