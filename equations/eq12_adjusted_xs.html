Equation 12: Compressed Cross Section

Equation:

\[\Sigma = \Sigma_0 \left(\frac{\rho}{\rho_0}\right) \left(1 - BF + 2BF \frac{\sigma_{ff}}{\sigma_{fuel}}\right)\]

Variables:

  • \(\Sigma\): Compressed macroscopic cross section [cm-1]
  • \(\Sigma_0\): Initial macroscopic cross section [cm-1]
  • \(\rho\): Compressed density [g/cm3]
  • \(\rho_0\): Initial density [g/cm3]
  • \(BF\): Burn-up factor [-]
  • \(\sigma_{ff}\): Fission fragment cross section [barns or cm2]
  • \(\sigma_{fuel}\): Fuel cross section [barns or cm2]

Description:

This equation accounts for the change in any macroscopic cross section when nuclear material is compressed and burned, considering both density changes and burn-up effects.
