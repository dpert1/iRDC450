Equation 21: Planck Group Integral

Equation:

\[P_g = \int_{h\nu_{g-1}}^{h\nu_g} \frac{15}{(\pi k T)^4} \frac{(h\nu)^3}{\exp\left(\frac{h\nu}{kT}\right) - 1} d(h\nu)\]

Variables:

  • \(P_g\): Fraction of blackbody radiation in energy group g [-]
  • \(h\nu\): Photon energy [eV]
  • \(h\nu_g\): Upper energy boundary of group g [eV]
  • \(h\nu_{g-1}\): Lower energy boundary of group g [eV]
  • \(k\): Boltzmann constant [eV/K]
  • \(T\): Temperature [K]

Description:

Integration of the Planck distribution over an energy group to determine the fraction of blackbody radiation in that group for multi-group transport calculations.
