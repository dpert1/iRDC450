Equation 1: Separative Work Units (SWU)

Equation:

\[SWU = [P \cdot V(x_p) + W \cdot V(x_w) - F \cdot V(x_f)] \tau \quad \text{[kg-SWU]}\]

Variables:

  • \(SWU\): Separative work units [kg-SWU]
  • \(P\): Product flow rate [kg/time]
  • \(W\): Waste (tails) flow rate [kg/time]
  • \(F\): Feed flow rate [kg/time]
  • \(V(x_i)\): Separation potential \(x_i\) [SWU]
  • \(x_p\): Product enrichment (mass fraction) [-]
  • \(x_w\): Waste enrichment (mass fraction) [-]
  • \(x_f\): Feed enrichment (mass fraction) [-]
  • \(\tau\): Time period [time]

Description:

Separative work units quantify the effort required in isotope separation processes. The separation potential, V(x), represents the work needed to enrich material to a given concentration. Generally, we will consider time to be unity, so \(\tau\) = 1 and the flow rates will become masses by unit cancellation.
