Equation 43: Mass Attenuation Coefficient (Mixture)

Equation:

\[\frac{\mu}{\rho} = \frac{1}{100} \sum_{i=1}^{n} \omega_i \left(\frac{\mu}{\rho}\right)_i \quad \left[\frac{\text{cm}^2}{\text{g}}\right]\]

Variables:

  • \(\mu/\rho\): Mass attenuation coefficient of mixture [cm2/g]
  • \(\omega_i\): Weight percent of component i [%]
  • \((\mu/\rho)_i\): Mass attenuation coefficient of component i [cm2/g]
  • \(n\): Number of components [-]

Description:

Mixture rule for calculating mass attenuation coefficients from constituent materials weighted by their mass fractions. The factor of 1/100 converts weight percent to weight fraction.
